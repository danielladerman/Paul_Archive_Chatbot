# People Glossary for Rabbi Paul S. Laderman Z"L

This file is a structured glossary of people and communities connected in some way to Rabbi Paul S. Laderman Z"L.  
Each entry keeps the details from the original tribute document, emphasizing how each person or group relates to Paul and his life.

---

## Immediate Family

- **Bess Laderman** – Paul's mother, from the Malin family of Kansas City. Born in Russia, she fled with her mother and younger sister on a 10‑year journey through Europe and Cuba (two years there) before arriving in the USA. She founded the Denver Symphony and spearheaded the Denver–Carmiel Israel Sister City program.
- **Rabbi Manual (Manny) Laderman** – Paul's father, born in New York City. Became Denver's first modern Orthodox rabbi in 1932 and was an active interfaith leader in Denver, Colorado, serving as head of the Denver Health Board.
- **Paul Laderman** – Born August 23, 1934 at 6 pounds 9 ounces, premature and sickly; he received blood transfusions at 10 months old.
- **Mierle (Miry) Laderman Ukeles** – Paul's sister, born September 25, 1939 (when Paul was 5) in Denver, weighing 9 pounds 3 ounces. Five years younger than Paul, she became a world‑renowned artist who created "Maintenance Art," triggered by her 1969 Manifesto. She married Dr. Jack Ukeles in 1965 and they have three children: Yael, Raquel (Raqi), and Meir.
  - **Yael Ukeles** – Paul's niece, daughter of Miry; she has two sons, **Amitai** and **Adir**.
  - **Raquel (Raqi) Ukeles** – Paul's niece; married **Marc Grossman**. They have three children: **Eitan**, **Rebecca**, and **Shai**.
  - **Meir Ukeles** – Paul's nephew; married **Pamela**, and they have three children: **Yadin**, **Sophia**, and **Amalia**.
- **Shula Jackson Laderman** – Paul's wife of over 55 years. Born in Jerusalem in 1940. She met Paul on a blind date in Times Square, New York, in 1958. Shula worked as a teacher and computer programmer, then began a career in art history at age 40, earned her PhD, and continues to publish and teach comparative art history.
- **Zev Laderman** – Paul's eldest son, born in Denver, Colorado, in 1961. Married to Noga in 1993. They have two sons (Yonatan and Daniel).
- **Noga Eshban Laderman** - Married to Zev in 1993. They have two sons: Yonatan, and Daniel.
- **Yonatan Manual Laderman** - born 2000 in Tel Aviv, Israel. 
- **Daniel B. Laderman** - born 2002 in Jerusalem.
- **Ilana Laderman Mushkin** – Paul's daughter, born in Denver, Colorado, in 1962. Married **Zachi Mushkin** in 1988.
  - **Keren Mushkin** – Paul's granddaughter, born 1990; married **Doron Kuperman**, and they have two children, **Meshy** and **Itai**.
  - **Eitan Mushkin** – Paul's grandson, born 1993; married **Gabi Beygel**, and they have a child **Keshet**.
  - **Shimri Mushkin** – Paul's granddaughter; married **Matan Gal**.
  - **Roie Mushkin** – Shimri’s twin brother, born in 1997, also Paul's grandson.
- **Rafael (Rafi) Laderman** – Paul's youngest son, born in Oakland, California, in August 1965. Married **Chani Shumer** in 2005.
  - **Mayan Laderman** – Rafi’s daughter (mother: **Liat**, Rafi’s first wife), born 1997; to marry **Dan Freinlander** in December 2025.
  - **Milka** – Chani’s daughter (father: **Dudy**, Chani’s first husband), born 1997; married **Harel**, with two children **Hadar** and **Baeri**.
  - **Elkana Shumer** – Son of Dudy and Chani, born 2000; married **Noa**, with a child **Arbel**.
  - **Avia** – Child in Rafi and Chani’s blended family, born 2006.
  - **Orie** – Youngest child in Rafi and Chani’s blended family, born 2011.

## Extended Family – Kansas City (Malin Family)

- **Moshe Malin** – Paul's grandfather (maternal side). Arrived in Kansas City in 1912, fleeing Czarist Russia.
- **Libby Malin** – Moshe's wife and Paul's grandmother; arrived in 1922 with her two daughters after a dangerous journey through Europe.
- **Betty Malin Solomon** – Paul's aunt; Bess's younger sister by two years. Came to America with Bess in 1923 and married **Rabbi Maurice Solomon**.
- **Rabbi Maurice Solomon (Uncle Mory)** – Paul's uncle by marriage and Manual Laderman's Yeshiva best friend. Served as rabbi at Kehilat Israel in Kansas City.
- **Ronald (Ronnie) Solomon** – Son of Uncle Mory and Aunt Betty; Paul's cousin, born in Kansas City in 1940. Married **Dianne**; they lived in New York City.
  - **Sheila** – Youngest of Ronnie's siblings; lives in Israel with her two children **Erik** and **Michael**.
  - **Leon** – Ronnie's brother; lives in Florida with two sons **Aaron** and **Gabriel**.
  - **Gloria Solomon Parelman** – Ronnie's sister; married **Alan Parelman** and lived in Kansas City. They had three children: **Joey**, **Julie**, and **Amy**. Gloria died in a tragic accident; Alan later married **Suzanne** and had a child **Ken Parelman**.
- **Melvin (Mel) Malin** – Paul's uncle; American‑born brother of Bess, born after Libby joined Moshe in the USA. Served as a Navy air navigator in World War II. Married **Lorie** in New York City; they settled in Kansas City and had three children:
  - **Larry Malin** – Married **Bridgette**.
  - **David Malin** – Married **Gayle**; they have two children **Netanaya Joy** and **Josh**.
  - **Lisa Malin** – Married **Dana Gibson**; they have two children **Max** and **Jacob (Jake)**.

## Family – Maternal Side (Malin) Direct Ancestors

- **Leib Malin** – Paul's great‑great‑grandfather; a devout Chassid from Zhivitov, a butcher and renowned scholar.
- **Chaim Malin** – Paul's great‑grandfather, one of Leib's five sons. Dealt in cattle and married **Golda Segal**; described as over six feet tall.
- **Golda Segal Malin** – Paul's great‑grandmother; under five feet tall. Died in Kansas City in 1934.
- **Bess Malin** – Paul's mother, born April 2, 1911 in Russia. Came to America in 1923 at age 12; finished elementary school in three years and high school in two years.

## Chicago Family (Laderman Relatives)

- **William (Zev) Laderman** – Paul's grandfather on his father's side; a civil‑rights advocate. Immigrated to New York from Nymerev, Ukraine, where Manual Laderman was born on Orchard Street. Moved to Chicago after his wife's death to be close to family; died December 5, 1941, and is buried in Chicago.
- **Manual Laderman** – Paul's father (also above). Lost his mother **Faiga (Fannie)** at age three in New York City and moved to Chicago with his father in 1912, growing up there.
- **Yocheved (Eve)** – Paul's great‑aunt; came to America with Paul's grandmother **Faiga (Fannie)**. Died in 1921 in Chicago. She had four daughters and one son with whom Manual grew up “as brothers and sisters.”
- **Mae** – Eldest daughter of Eve; married **Joe Gray**. They were Paul's grandfather's closest relatives, and Mae was like a sister to Manual when he moved to Chicago as a child.
  - **Avrum Gray** – Mae and Joe’s eldest son; was like a brother to Paul when Paul studied in Chicago and visited on weekends.
- **Bess** – Relative in the Chicago branch; had a daughter **Mimie Spector**, whose sons are **Howard** and **David Spector**.
- **Ida** – Another relative in the Chicago Laderman family network (details minimal in the source).
- **Arlene Feiger** – Cousin very close to Paul. He was her date to her high school prom. Married **Harry Feiger** and had three children: **Gayle** (married **Andy Brown**), **Jon** (married **Nance**), and **Rob** (married **Allyson**).
- **Jon and Rob Feiger** – Arlene’s sons; both spent junior years in Israel and were very close with Paul.
- **Bill Siskel** – Arlene’s younger brother, mentored by Paul. Married **Judy**; their sons are **Jon**, **Charlie**, and **Ed**.
- **Gene Siskel** – Bill’s older brother, famed film critic and family member, who died of the same GBM brain tumor about ten years before Paul.
- **Loseff Family** – **Judith**, **Bill**, and **Karen** Loseff; part of the extended Chicago family and social circle connected to Paul.

## Family – Paternal Side (Lederman/Laderman) Direct Ancestors

- **Hendel Menzalis** – Paul's maternal great‑great‑grandfather (through the Lederman/Laderman line), an innkeeper in Nemirov who married twice.
- **Fraida** – Hendel's first wife.
- **Schlome Menzalis** – Hendel's son; died in Russia in 1920 and had a daughter **Chana Chyke Raitchek**.
- **Chana Chyke Raitchek** – Lived in Mexico City; had two children. Part of Paul's extended paternal ancestry.
- **Yocheved (Eve)** – As above, came to America with Paul's grandmother, died in Chicago in 1921, with four daughters and one son.
- **Malka** – Died in Russia in 1937; remained unmarried (“spinster”).
- **Schmelke Lederman** – Paul's great‑grandfather; a handsome, bearded Jew and cattleman in Nemirov. Killed in the pogrom of 1921.
- **Gitiel** – Schmelke's wife and Paul's great‑grandmother; described as a good‑looking woman, died in 1918.
- **Velvel (William) Lederman** – Paul's grandfather; oldest of Schmelke's children. Came to America in 1905.
- **Faiga (Fannie)** – Paul's grandmother; grew up in Nemirov and married Velvel. She and Velvel were childless in Europe.
- **Rabbi Manual (Manny) Laderman** – Paul's father, born August 25, 1909 on New York City's Lower East Side. Ordained in 1932 at age 23 and became Denver's first non‑bearded Orthodox rabbi.

### Great‑Uncles and Aunts (Schmelke's Other Children)

- **Nahum** – Child of Schmelke; died in the 1921 pogrom. His wife was still living in Russia.
- **Kutzi** – Sibling who died young.
- **Schlome** – Another son of Schmelke; died in the same 1921 pogrom, leaving a surviving wife.
- **Breina** – Daughter of Schmelke; died during WWII in Siberia. She left three living children (one in the Russian army in Vladivostok, a daughter in Odessa, and a son in Viniztek).
- **Selig** – Youngest child of Schmelke; came to America with Velvel in 1905.

## Shula Jackson Laderman’s Family (Jackson/Schmida)

- **Tirza Jackson (née Schmida)** – Shula's mother and Paul's mother‑in‑law. Born in Bratislava, Slovakia, she immigrated overland to Palestine in 1929 with her siblings.
- **Isaac Jackson** – Shula's father and Paul's father‑in‑law. Born in Cork, Ireland; immigrated with his family to the USA. Most of his family settled in Georgia, including the Jackson and Mescon families.
- **Yehoshua “Jaky” Jackson** – Shula's oldest brother, born in Jerusalem in 1938. Married **Negba** and has one child **Yael** plus another child **Yossi** from a previous marriage.
- **Yossi “Jacksi” Jackson** – Shula's younger brother; married **Hanita** and had three children (**Guy** – married **Anat**, **Ron**, and **Laurie**). After Hanita’s passing he married **Tzvia**.
- **Nurit Jackson Sinai** – Shula's younger sister, born in Jerusalem in 1948. Married **Nissim Sinai**; they have four children: **Ron**, **Tali**, **Hila**, and **Yitzik**.

### Shula’s Distant Maternal Family (Schmida)

- **Jacob Shmida** – Tirza’s eldest brother; married **Leah** and had three children: **Chava (?)**, **Elchanan**, and **Naama**.
- **Moshe Shmida** – Tirza’s brother; married **Miryam** and had three children: **Rafi**, **Osnat**, and **Ariela**.
- **Shlomo Shmida** – Tirza’s brother (called “small Shlomo Shmida”); some descendant details remain incomplete in the source.
- **Rivka Shmida (Rivka Elitzur)** – Tirza’s sister; married **Professor Yehuda Elitzur**. They had four children:
  - **Professor Shmuel Elitzur** – Married **Zahava**; he stayed up all night with Zev after Paul's death to honor Paul's soul.
  - **Tamar Elitzur**
  - **Uri Elitzur** – Married **Matti**.
  - **Vered Elitzur** – Married **Noam**.
  Rivka died at an early age. After **Isaac Jackson** died, Professor Yehuda Elitzur and Tirza later married.
- **Margalit** – Relative who died at an early age. She had two daughters:
  - **Ruttie** – Married **Uri** and had two sons **Gad** and **Peled**.
  - **Leah** – Married **Avram** and had four children **Adi**, **Dan**, **Rakefet**, and **Galit**.
- **Zev (Tirza’s father)** – Had a brother **Itzchak** in Prague, the cantor at the Altneuschul. Zev urged him to immigrate to Palestine, but Itzchak stayed to shepherd his community. He was taken by the Nazis in 1942 to Theresienstadt, where he worked on the Jewish archives the Nazis wanted to create, and in 1944 he was sent to Auschwitz and murdered.
  - **Tall Shlomo Shmida** – Eldest son of Itzchak, distinguished from Tirza’s brother “small Shlomo.” On Yom Kippur 1941 he was taken from his father’s side to a youth detention camp, fled, joined the partisans until the end of the war, and then immigrated to Israel. He became Paul's Jewish Agency advisor in Israel (1957–58) and later married and had children including **Zachi** and **Rutie**.

### Shula’s Distant Paternal Family

- **Isaac Jackson** – As above, Shula's father; much of his extended family settled in Georgia.
- **Enid Jackson** – Daughter of Isaac’s sister who immigrated to Atlanta; Enid strongly resembled Tirza, and people often confused the two. She married **Professor Mike Mescon**; they had three children **Tim**, **Jedd**, and **Nancy**. Paul and Shula were very close to Mike and Enid, and Paul officiated **Tim Mescon**'s wedding in Atlanta in 1983.

## Friends and Colleagues

### Denver

- **Paul Melmed** – Grew up next door to Paul on Newton Street in Denver. Later moved to the Bay Area for graduate school and remained a lifelong friend. Married **Ronny**; their children include:
  - **Carie** – Married **Ben Goorin**; they have three children **Cassie**, **Noah**, and **Zane**.
  - **David**
  - **Zane** – Married **Virginia**; Zane died shortly before Paul from the same GBM brain tumor. Four generations of kinship developed between the Laderman and Zerobnick families.
- **Jordan Perlmutter and Charlene Sachter** – Childhood friends of Paul who both passed away in 2015.
  - Their children include **Jonathan Perlmutter**, **Vickey Dansky**, and **Sammie Sachter Hassan**, who chanted Kaddish with Zev.
- **Senator John Carroll** – U.S. Senator from Colorado, family friend; may have arranged Paul's Denver military assignment.

### Berkeley / California Era

- **Rabbi Shlomo Carlebach** – Partnered with Paul in making Judaism relevant to the hippie and liberal generation; created the House of Love and Prayer.
- **Rabbi Yosef Langer** – Initial Chabad rabbi of San Francisco; Paul helped him when he was lost in the 1960s.
- **Congressman Ronald Dellums** – First Black Congressman from Northern California; maintained a close friendship with Paul.
- **Al Davis** – Oakland Raiders owner/founder. Paul befriended him and officiated his son **Mark Davis**'s bar mitzvah.
- **Rabbi Benji Segal** – Lifelong friend and co‑conspirator with Paul in activism against Governor Reagan. Made aliyah with Paul in 1973.
- **Ruven Stein** – Dear lifelong friend; also made aliyah from the Bay Area with Paul and was Paul's running partner in the hills of Jerusalem for decades. Stayed up all night with Zev after Paul's death.
- **Joel and Irene Resnekolf** – Neighbors and friends of Paul and Shula in Berkeley, California.

### Israel

- **Rabbi Reuven Hammer** – Fellow rabbi who met Paul at Rodef Shalom in Denver during Paul's Lowry AFB tenure. Made aliyah at the same time as Paul and became a lifelong friend and colleague, eventually leading the Conservative movement in Israel.
- **Rabbi Lionel Rosenfeld** – Dear friend and neighbor in Jerusalem who later became a renowned chazzan and was ordained as a rabbi. Married **Natalie (Nataly)**; Lionel sang mizmorim for Paul in his final days.
- **Yossi Harel** – Eternal gabbai of Young Israel synagogue in Armon Hanatziv, Jerusalem. Had a special relationship with Paul; they studied Talmud together daily.
- **Dr. Harry Muzlow (Dr. Zvi ben Itzchak)** – Came to Israel on a junior year abroad and became the devoted doctor who cared for Paul throughout his life.
- **Steve and Rebecca Zerobnick** – Paul officiated their wedding in Miami roughly 30 years before his passing. Their family represents four generations of kinship between the Laderman and Zerobnick families.

### Academic / Rabbinical Mentors

- **Rabbi Joseph B. Soloveitchik** – Major rabbinic teacher under whom Paul studied at Yeshiva University Seminary; he ordained Paul.
- **Rabbi Samuel Belkin** – Co‑ordained Paul at Yeshiva University.
- **Rabbi Kuk (nephew of the famous Rabbi Kook)** – Head of Mercaz HaRav Kook in 1957–58, where Paul studied. Provided Paul with a letter of distinction.

### Historical / Inspirational Figures in Paul’s Life

- **Reverend Archibald Carey** – Paul's grandfather's Black childhood friend from school and a civil‑rights leader, who originally authored a version of the “I Have a Dream” speech in 1952. A key inspiration for Paul.
- **Jackie Robinson** – Baseball legend. Paul and cousin **Arlene** witnessed prejudice against Robinson at a Chicago Cubs game, inspiring them to become civil‑rights activists.
- **Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.** – Paul had planned to march with him in Selma in 1965 but canceled due to Shula's late pregnancy.
- **Golda Myerson (Golda Meir)** – Future Prime Minister of Israel; Paul and Miry obtained her autograph in Paris in 1948 at the United Nations.
- **Ralph Bunche** – U.S. Ambassador to the UN and Nobel Peace Prize laureate; Paul witnessed him testify at a committee hearing in Paris in 1948.
- **An Israeli Policeman (unnamed)** – When 15‑year‑old Paul was lost in Israel in 1949, this policeman reassured him: “You are not lost at all, you are in Israel, the land of the Jews; this is your home.”

## Communities and Organizations Connected to Paul

- **Radical Jewish Union at Berkeley** – Community Paul supported and helped build, reflecting his openness to radical Jewish pluralism.
- **Chabad of Berkeley** – Early Chabad presence in Berkeley, with four initial shluchim sent by the Lubavitcher Rebbe in 1971; Paul embraced them and helped build their community.
- **Ethiopian Jews / American Association for Ethiopian Jews** – Paul served as Israeli director of the American Association for Ethiopian Jews in 1985, helping to resettle over 50,000 Ethiopian Jews.
- **Kaifeng China Jewish Family** – Paul helped with the absorption and integration of this Jewish family from Kaifeng, China, into Israel.
- **Oz Veshalom and Netivot Shalom** – Religious peace movements Paul joined in 1986, promoting peace based on Jewish ethical principles.
- **Meimad Movement** – Moderate left‑wing religious political party that Paul helped found in 1988 to promote peace between Jews and Arabs.

## Historical and Religious Figures (From Paul’s Writings and Life Story)

### Ancient / Historical

- **Rabbi Yehiel Mihiel** – Historical leader of the Jewish community in Nemirov, brutally burned at the stake during the 1648 pogrom.
- **Rabbi Yom Tov Lipman Heller** – Author of the Mishnah commentary *Tosefot Yom Tov*, who in 1642 is said to have prophesied the 1648 pogrom. Served as rabbi at the Altneuschul in Prague.
- **Bohdan Chmielnicki (Chmielnitzke)** – Cossack leader who initiated the 1648 revolt and associated pogroms that devastated Jewish communities in Eastern Europe.
- **Baal Shem Tov** – Founder of Hasidism (Pietism), born around 1700 in Okop, Podolia, Ukraine; a spiritual ancestor whose movement shaped much of the Jewish world Paul inherited.

### School, Youth, and Teachers

- **Roberta Toltz** – Paul's subordinate / assistant editor at *Colfax Vacuum*; she greeted Paul at the airport on his return from Europe, symbolizing the network of support around him.
- **Burton Weiner** – Friend who had a memorable water fight with Paul at Camp Moshava.
- **Bernie Nouman** – Friend who desecrated the Sabbath to drive Paul to the doctor after a serious glass accident and patiently waited with him at the hospital.

### School / Academic Figures – Paris

- **Monsieur Paul G. de Rosay** – Graduate of Boston Latin School and Harvard; headmaster of the American Community School of Paris while Paul was there.

### Political and Public Figures Encountered

#### United Nations / Government Officials

- **Dr. Ralph Bunche** – U.S. Ambassador to the UN (descended from enslaved ancestors over four generations). Paul saw him testify on Palestine mediation; Bunche was a chain smoker and a major world figure Paul admired.
- **Golda Myerson (later Meir)** – Testified at the UN Political Committee on Palestine; Paul and Miry secured her autograph, marking an early brush with future Israeli leadership.
- **President Harry S. Truman** – U.S. president who won the 1948 election; Paul was in Kansas City during the election and felt his presence as part of that historic moment.

#### British Officials

- **Ernest Bevin** – British Foreign Secretary; Paul saw him on a ship and obtained his autograph.
- **Sir Ernest Bevin's Aide** – Man carrying a “His Majesty's Royal Foreign Office” briefcase marked “top secret,” glimpsed by Paul and remembered as part of his encounters with power.

#### Israeli Leaders

- **Rabbi Meir Berlin** – President of the Mizrachi world religious movement and the Hapoel Hamizrachi labor movement. Hosted Paul's family for their first Passover meal in Israel and died shortly thereafter.
- **Rabbi Yitzhak Herzog** – Chief Rabbi of Israel; hosted Paul's family for a Friday night meal, a formative spiritual experience.
- **Rabbi Maimon** – Minister of Religions of the State of Israel.
- **Mr. Shragai** – Brain behind Hapoel Hamizrachi and mayor of Jerusalem.
- **Mr. Shapiro** – Official Paul met at Hakirya, the temporary capital of Israel.
- **Isaac Mayer Levin** – Minister of Health and Sanitation in the early State of Israel.
- **James G. McDonald** – American Ambassador to the State of Israel; described by Paul as “a fascinating man who came to be a leading world figure by a series of coincidences.”

### Entertainment and Cultural Figures

- **Jack Benny** – Movie star Paul saw aboard the *Queen Elizabeth* ship.
- **Lana Turner** – Movie star Paul saw aboard the *Queen Mary*.
- **Dennis Morgan** – Movie star Paul saw aboard the *Queen Mary*.
- **Paul Paray** – French conductor of the Israeli Orchestra, who lived a block from Paul's family in Tel Aviv.
- **Danny Kaye** – Movie star whose film Paul watched in Kansas City during the Truman election.

### Other Notable People

- **Tatiana Roswell** – Miry’s school friend and first cousin of the Greek king, reflecting the wide range of people around Paul's family.
- **The Greek King** – Monarch whom Paul watched inspecting American soldiers; appeared drunk in Paul's recollection.
- **Eddie Jacobson** – President Truman's close friend and former clothing‑store partner. Attended a reception at Aunt Betty's house, connecting Paul's family to the highest levels of American political life.
