# People Glossary for Rabbi Paul S. Laderman Z"L

This file is a structured glossary of people and communities connected in some way to Rabbi Paul S. Laderman Z"L.  
Each entry keeps the details from the original tribute document, emphasizing how each person or group relates to Paul and his life.

---

## Immediate Family

### Academic / Rabbinical Mentors

- **Rabbi Samuel Belkin** – Co‑ordained Paul at Yeshiva University.

### Historical / Inspirational Figures in Paul’s Life

- **Reverend Archibald Carey** – Paul's grandfather's Black childhood friend from school and a civil‑rights leader, who originally authored a version of the “I Have a Dream” speech in 1952. A key inspiration for Paul.
- **Jackie Robinson** – Baseball legend. Paul and cousin **Arlene** witnessed prejudice against Robinson at a Chicago Cubs game, inspiring them to become civil‑rights activists.
- **Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.** – Paul had planned to march with him in Selma in 1965 but canceled due to Shula's late pregnancy.
- **Golda Myerson (Golda Meir)** – Future Prime Minister of Israel; Paul and Miry obtained her autograph in Paris in 1948 at the United Nations.
- **Ralph Bunche** – U.S. Ambassador to the UN and Nobel Peace Prize laureate; Paul witnessed him testify at a committee hearing in Paris in 1948.
- **An Israeli Policeman (unnamed)** – When 15‑year‑old Paul was lost in Israel in 1949, this policeman reassured him: “You are not lost at all, you are in Israel, the land of the Jews; this is your home.”

## Communities and Organizations Connected to Paul

- **Radical Jewish Union at Berkeley** – Community Paul supported and helped build, reflecting his openness to radical Jewish pluralism.
- **Chabad of Berkeley** – Early Chabad presence in Berkeley, with four initial shluchim sent by the Lubavitcher Rebbe in 1971; Paul embraced them and helped build their community.
- **Ethiopian Jews / American Association for Ethiopian Jews** – Paul served as Israeli director of the American Association for Ethiopian Jews in 1985, helping to resettle over 50,000 Ethiopian Jews.
- **Kaifeng China Jewish Family** – Paul helped with the absorption and integration of this Jewish family from Kaifeng, China, into Israel.
- **Oz Veshalom and Netivot Shalom** – Religious peace movements Paul joined in 1986, promoting peace based on Jewish ethical principles.
- **Meimad Movement** – Moderate left‑wing religious political party that Paul helped found in 1988 to promote peace between Jews and Arabs.

## Historical and Religious Figures (From Paul’s Writings and Life Story)

### Ancient / Historical

- **Rabbi Yehiel Mihiel** – Historical leader of the Jewish community in Nemirov, brutally burned at the stake during the 1648 pogrom.
- **Rabbi Yom Tov Lipman Heller** – Author of the Mishnah commentary *Tosefot Yom Tov*, who in 1642 is said to have prophesied the 1648 pogrom. Served as rabbi at the Altneuschul in Prague.
- **Bohdan Chmielnicki (Chmielnitzke)** – Cossack leader who initiated the 1648 revolt and associated pogroms that devastated Jewish communities in Eastern Europe.
- **Baal Shem Tov** – Founder of Hasidism (Pietism), born around 1700 in Okop, Podolia, Ukraine; a spiritual ancestor whose movement shaped much of the Jewish world Paul inherited.

### School, Youth, and Teachers

- **Roberta Toltz** – Paul's subordinate / assistant editor at *Colfax Vacuum*; she greeted Paul at the airport on his return from Europe, symbolizing the network of support around him.
- **Burton Weiner** – Friend who had a memorable water fight with Paul at Camp Moshava.
- **Bernie Nouman** – Friend who desecrated the Sabbath to drive Paul to the doctor after a serious glass accident and patiently waited with him at the hospital.

### School / Academic Figures – Paris

- **Monsieur Paul G. de Rosay** – Graduate of Boston Latin School and Harvard; headmaster of the American Community School of Paris while Paul was there.

### Political and Public Figures Encountered

#### United Nations / Government Officials

- **Dr. Ralph Bunche** – U.S. Ambassador to the UN (descended from enslaved ancestors over four generations). Paul saw him testify on Palestine mediation; Bunche was a chain smoker and a major world figure Paul admired.
- **Golda Myerson (later Meir)** – Testified at the UN Political Committee on Palestine; Paul and Miry secured her autograph, marking an early brush with future Israeli leadership.
- **President Harry S. Truman** – U.S. president who won the 1948 election; Paul was in Kansas City during the election and felt his presence as part of that historic moment.

#### British Officials

- **Ernest Bevin** – British Foreign Secretary; Paul saw him on a ship and obtained his autograph.
- **Sir Ernest Bevin's Aide** – Man carrying a “His Majesty's Royal Foreign Office” briefcase marked “top secret,” glimpsed by Paul and remembered as part of his encounters with power.

#### Israeli Leaders

- **Rabbi Meir Berlin** – President of the Mizrachi world religious movement and the Hapoel Hamizrachi labor movement. Hosted Paul's family for their first Passover meal in Israel and died shortly thereafter.
- **Rabbi Yitzhak Herzog** – Chief Rabbi of Israel; hosted Paul's family for a Friday night meal, a formative spiritual experience.
- **Rabbi Maimon** – Minister of Religions of the State of Israel.
- **Mr. Shragai** – Brain behind Hapoel Hamizrachi and mayor of Jerusalem.
- **Mr. Shapiro** – Official Paul met at Hakirya, the temporary capital of Israel.
- **Isaac Mayer Levin** – Minister of Health and Sanitation in the early State of Israel.
- **James G. McDonald** – American Ambassador to the State of Israel; described by Paul as “a fascinating man who came to be a leading world figure by a series of coincidences.”
